## 11. Outlook built-in read and delivery receipts (Microsoft)

Sometimes the simplest solution is built right into the tools you already use. Outlook’s native read and delivery receipts offer a foundational level of tracking without any third-party add-ins. A delivery receipt confirms the email reached the recipient's mail server, while a read receipt requests confirmation when they open it. This makes it a straightforward, if limited, option for those seeking the **best Outlook email tracking** without adding new software.

![Outlook built-in read and delivery receipts (Microsoft)](https://cdn.outrank.so/f45919cb-f824-4ceb-a837-e4aa19dc066c/screenshots/55d5c241-c1bf-42fe-aca9-70d8b4807a83.jpg)

This feature is best for internal communications or situations where you only need basic confirmation. Unlike pixel-based tracking, its biggest drawback is that the recipient must consent to sending the read receipt, and many email clients or security-conscious users will decline. It provides a simple "yes" or "no" on delivery and (potential) open, but lacks the granular data like link clicks or multiple opens that RevOps teams rely on for engagement analysis.

### Key Features & Implementation

*   **Core Tracking:** Per-message or global settings for requesting delivery and read receipts.
*   **Workflow Integration:** Native functionality included directly within all modern versions of Outlook, including web and desktop.
*   **Reporting:** Simple email notifications confirm delivery or receipt; there are no aggregated dashboards or analytics.

Implementation is as simple as it gets. You can enable receipts for individual messages under the "Options" tab before sending or set it as a global default in Outlook’s settings. No installation is required, making it instantly available for any user.

> **Why It's a Top Pick:** It’s the most accessible form of tracking available, completely free and built-in. For organizations with strict IT policies that prohibit third-party add-ins, this native function provides a basic, consent-based alternative for confirming message delivery.

**Pricing:** Free. This functionality is included with your Microsoft 365 or Outlook account at no extra cost.
