## Exploring Common RevOps Team Models

A well-defined RevOps structure acts like a GPS for your growth journey. By picturing different setups, you can align your team model with your company’s stage and culture.

### Centralized Model

Think of a centralized team as a symphony orchestra, all playing from the same sheet music under one conductor. Every process—campaign execution, pipeline hygiene, forecasting—follows a uniform playbook.

For instance, a B2B SaaS firm with **$30M ARR** assembled a five-person centralized RevOps squad reporting to the VP of RevOps. They handled everything from demand generation to revenue analysis in a single hub.

Key perks:  
- **Streamlined decision making** thanks to one clear line of sight  
- **Consistent metrics** and handoffs across all functions  

Keep in mind, this setup can feel rigid when product lines demand bespoke strategies.

### Pod Model

Pods resemble sports teams—small, cross-functional units that huddle around specific plays. Each pod blends marketing, sales, customer success, and analytics pros focused on a particular segment.

A mid-market company spun up three pods targeting enterprise, SMB, and channel partners. These mini-teams owned end-to-end metrics and pivoted quickly on niche campaigns.

Pod advantages:  
- **Hyper-focused** attention on each segment drives better conversion  
- **Rapid experimentation** lets teams react instantly to market feedback  
- **Heightened ownership** fuels accountability  

Yet pods can lead to duplicated roles and occasional misalignment between squads.

### Hybrid Model

Picture a network of specialist hubs linked by shared resources. The hybrid model weaves together centralized expertise and embedded analysts.

A $100M ARR SaaS leader kept a core team of data engineers and enablement leads, while placing two analysts in every product line. This structure offered both the rigor of a central team and the responsiveness of pods.

Hybrid highlights:  
- **Balanced oversight** with local agility  
- **Efficient resource sharing** between central and embedded experts  

> **Cross-Functional Alignment Drives Measurable Impact**  
> Centralized metrics and joint processes can lift revenue by double digits.
